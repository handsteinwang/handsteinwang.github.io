Posted 2025-01-29Investment and Trends

Pharmaceutical Trends

Recently, the issue of imported medicines withdrawing from public hospitals has attracted widespread attention and has repeatedly topped trending search rankings. Foreign pharmaceutical companies have become increasingly pessimistic about the Chinese market, and business contraction, layoffs, and asset sales have become ever more frequent. December 12, 2024 may be regarded as a critical turning point. On that day, the results of what the industry described as the most unprecedented round in history—the tenth batch of the National Centralized Volume-Based Pharmaceutical Procurement—were announced. In other words, the centralized procurement results were released, and all companies producing originator medicines either declined to participate directly or merely submitted token quotations as a formality. In short, the final outcome was that not a single originator-medicine company won the bid. In January of this year, many people discovered when seeking treatment at hospitals that imported medicines previously supplied on a stable basis had suddenly gone out of stock. Imported medicines are now becoming increasingly difficult to obtain, and in the future they may no longer be available for prescription at all, leaving patients with no option but to switch midway to domestic medicines. Imported medicines seem to have disappeared in this silent manner.

Local Convex Topological Vector Spaces

In revisiting the proofs of many theorems in mathematics, we often need to use sequential compactness. However, the Riesz lemma in normed linear spaces tells us that every bounded set in an infinite-dimensional normed linear space is not sequentially compact, which brings many inconveniences. In fact, the essential reason for this phenomenon is that the topology induced by the norm in a normed linear space is “too strong.” For this reason, we need to introduce the weak topology, so that bounded sets are sequentially compact in the weak-topology sense. In this article, we mainly review local convex topological vector spaces and some main results on weak convergence.

Note: Unless otherwise specified, all normed linear spaces in this article are understood to be complex normed linear spaces.

Posted 2024-12-02Probability Theory

Probability Measure on the Path Space

Stochastic Processes and Paths

Definition 1: Let $(\Omega,\mathscr{F},\mathbb{P})$ be a probability space, and let $(X_t)_{t\in T}$ be a family of random variables on $(\Omega,\mathscr{F},\mathbb{P})$, that is, for every $t\in T$, we have
$$
X_t: (\Omega,\mathscr{F})\to (\mathbb{R},\mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}))
$$
measurable. Then
$$
\begin{aligned}
X:T\times\Omega&\to \mathbb{R}\\\
(t,\omega)&\mapsto X_t(\omega)
\end{aligned}
$$
is called a stochastic process. For fixed $\omega\in \Omega$, we call
$$
\begin{aligned}
X_\omega:T&\to \mathbb{R}\\\
t&\mapsto X(t,\omega)
\end{aligned}
$$
a path, and call the collection of all paths
$$
\mathbb{D} := \{X_\omega\ ;\ \omega\in \Omega\}
$$
the path space.

Remark: Each element $X_\omega$ of the path space is a mapping from $T$ to $\mathbb{R}$.
